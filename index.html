Who Am I?

I'm Matt—a Mechatronics Engineering student who's obsessed with building things. Robots, software, hardware, startups, you name it. I've probably tried to automate it or make it smarter.

My philosophy is simple: learn by doing. Want to understand robotics? Build one. Curious about AI? Train a model. Wondering how something works? Take it apart and rebuild it better.

I've gone from building my first sketchy robotic arm to designing tactile sensors, training VLA models, building acoustic test facilities, and shipping hardware that actually works. Every project teaches me something new. Every failure is just data.

I want to work on hard mechanical and robotics problems — the kind where the software, the hardware, and the manufacturing all have to come together at once.

The Rest of It

I've been building physical things since I was 8. Welding, machining, wiring, fabricating. Not everything makes it into a polished case study, but it all stacks up.

I built my first dune buggy at 15 from salvaged parts. Welded the frame, wired the ignition, drove it. That's where I learned tolerances, fabrication, and what it feels like when a mechanical system actually works. Since then: Tesla turbines, spot welders, rocket engines, a garage-built anechoic chamber, LED matrices with custom embedded drivers, and more robotic arms than I can count. I've worked with lathes, mills, TIG and MIG welders, and anything else I could get my hands on. I also built Portek AI, a video generation platform, and Cibus, a full-stack nutrition SaaS. But the hardware is where I'm most at home.

Fabrication

Dune Buggy

Built a fully functional off-road buggy at 15 from salvaged parts. Welded the frame, designed the drivetrain, wired the ignition. My first real mechanical build.

Mechanical

Tesla Turbine

Bladeless turbine running on compressed air. Machined the discs, designed the housing, tested thrust output. Deep dive into disc-based fluid mechanics.

Manufacturing

Spot Welder

High-current spot welder built from a salvaged microwave transformer. Rewound the secondary coil, built the electrodes, used it to weld battery packs and sheet metal.

Propulsion

Rocket Engine

Designed and test-fired a solid-propellant rocket engine from scratch. Worked through nozzle geometry, propellant grain design, and thrust curves.

Embedded

LED Matrix Displays

Large-format LED matrices with custom embedded drivers and real-time animations. Wrote the firmware, designed the PCB layout, built the panels myself.

Acoustics

Anechoic Chamber

Designed and built a 10x10x10 ft anechoic chamber in a garage for drone noise testing. Achieved 20 dB noise reduction. Structural design, acoustic foam layout, all from scratch.

AI / Software
