Tactile Sensors for Humanoids

Solving the chicken-and-egg problem in robotic touch

There's a chicken-and-egg problem in tactile sensing. We don't have good tactile data because we don't have good cheap sensors. We don't have good cheap sensors because nobody has figured out how to make them yet. And we don't have the models to make use of that data even if we did.

I'm building tactile sensors that are cheap enough to actually scale. Starting with the LeRobot SO-101 as the development platform, the goal is sensors that can be deployed across any humanoid or robot platform — not a one-off research prototype.

The vision: get real tactile data at scale, feed it back into models, and start closing that loop. Humanoids need touch to do useful work in the real world. That can't happen until the sensors are cheap, robust, and everywhere.
